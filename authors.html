Authors

Our authors are a diverse group of theologians, pastors, scholars, and Christian writers who have made significant contributions to the field of Christian theology and biblical studies. They come from various denominational backgrounds and theological traditions, but all share a commitment to faithful biblical interpretation and theological reflection.

Classic Theologians

The Classic Theologians category features influential figures from church history whose writings have shaped Christian thought and doctrine. These authors include early church fathers, medieval theologians, reformers, and other key figures in the development of Christian theology.

Wayne Grudem

Wayne Grudem is a prominent evangelical theologian known for his work in systematic theology and biblical studies. He has written extensively on topics such as the doctrine of God, Christology, and soteriology.

John Calvin

John Calvin was a 16th-century French theologian and pastor who played a key role in the Protestant Reformation. His theological writings, particularly his Institutes of the Christian Religion, have had a profound influence on Reformed theology.

John Stott

John Stott was an English Anglican priest and theologian known for his clear and faithful exposition of Christian doctrine. His works on the cross of Christ and Christian living have been widely read and appreciated by Christians around the world.

Contemporary Authors

The Contemporary Authors category features modern theologians, pastors, and Christian writers who are actively contributing to theological discourse and biblical scholarship today. These authors engage with contemporary issues facing the church and society while remaining rooted in biblical truth.

N.T. Wright

N.T. Wright is a leading New Testament scholar and theologian known for his work on the historical Jesus, Pauline theology, and Christian origins. His writings have had a significant impact on contemporary biblical scholarship and Christian thought.

Timothy Keller

Timothy Keller is a pastor and author known for his ability to communicate complex theological ideas in a clear and accessible way. His books on Christian apologetics, faith and work, and Christian living have been widely read and appreciated by Christians of all backgrounds.

Alister McGrath

Alister McGrath is a theologian, historian, and apologist known for his work on the relationship between science and faith, as well as his contributions to systematic theology. His writings explore the intersection of theology, philosophy, and culture.
